$$m= _{(n)}p$$
$$n\ |\ (m-p)$$
$$m-p=kn$$
As $k$ is an integer.

There must exist a k that satisfied:
$$
k = \lfloor \frac{m}{n} \rfloor - \lfloor \frac{p}{n} \rfloor
$$

\begin{alien*}

m-p&= (\lfloor \frac{m}{n} \rfloor - \lfloor \frac{p}{n} \rfloor) n\

m - \lfloor \frac{m}{n} \rfloor n = p - \lfloor \frac{p}{n} \rfloor n

m%n = p%n

\end{alien*}
