例题

A single die is tossed; then nnn coins are tossed where nnn is the number shown on the die. Show that the probability of exactly two heads is close to 0.2578.
参考答案
We will sum up the probabilities of getting exactly two heads for each possible outcome of the die roll (1 through 6). Assume the first number rolled was NNN.

P(X=2)=P(X=2∩N=1)+P(X=2∩N=2)+P(X=2∩N=3)+P(X=2∩N=4)+P(X=2∩N=5)+P(X=2∩N=6)\begin{aligned} P(X=2) &= P(X=2 \cap N=1) + P(X=2 \cap N=2) + P(X=2 \cap N=3) \\ &+ P(X=2 \cap N=4) + P(X=2 \cap N=5) + P(X=2 \cap N=6) \end{aligned}P(X=2)​=P(X=2∩N=1)+P(X=2∩N=2)+P(X=2∩N=3)+P(X=2∩N=4)+P(X=2∩N=5)+P(X=2∩N=6)​

The probability of getting exactly two heads when tossing nnn coins can be found using the binomial probability formula, which is given by:

P(X=k)=(nk)pk(1−p)n−kP(X=k) =\tbinom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k} P(X=k)=(kn​)pk(1−p)n−k

(nk)\tbinom{n}{k}(kn​) is the binomial coefficient, which calculates the number of ways to choose kkk successes out of nnn trials.

  • ppp is the probability of success, for toss a coin which is 12\frac{1}{2}21​.
  • nnn is the number of trials.
  • kkk is the number of successes, in this question means two heads.
  • Only when n≥2n \geq 2n≥2, PPP makes sense.

Therefore, We can have

P(X=2)=P(X=2∩N=1)+P(X=2∩N=2)+P(X=2∩N=3)+P(X=2∩N=4)+P(X=2∩N=5)+P(X=2∩N=6)=16((22)(12)2+(32)(12)3+(42)(12)4+(52)(12)5+(62)(12)6)=0.2578\begin{aligned} P(X=2) &= P(X=2 \cap N=1) + P(X=2 \cap N=2) + P(X=2 \cap N=3) \\ &+ P(X=2 \cap N=4) + P(X=2 \cap N=5) + P(X=2 \cap N=6)\\ &= \frac{1}{6}\left( \tbinom{2}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^2 + \tbinom{3}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^3 + \tbinom{4}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^4 + \tbinom{5}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^5 + \tbinom{6}{2}(\frac{1}{2})^6\right)\\ &= 0.2578 \end{aligned}P(X=2)​=P(X=2∩N=1)+P(X=2∩N=2)+P(X=2∩N=3)+P(X=2∩N=4)+P(X=2∩N=5)+P(X=2∩N=6)=61​((22​)(21​)2+(23​)(21​)3+(24​)(21​)4+(25​)(21​)5+(26​)(21​)6)=0.2578​
